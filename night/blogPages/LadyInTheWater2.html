Lady in the Water, now
⇦
⇨

I think it’s always a bad sign when you can’t tell what kind of movie you’re watching.

I want to point out first that Lady in the Water has obvious, glaring issues - and not just cinematic ones. The film includes stereotypical depictions of Asian and Latinx people, Paul Giamatti putting on a fake stutter, and a female main character (a young Bryce Dallas Howard) who rarely speaks.

To talk about the movie in any seriousness is tough. Giamatti’s forced efforts at sincerity are unbearable, and Shyamalan’s own cameo paints himself as a savior of an entire nation.

I want to believe that this movie was made for some other reason - like my dad did. Yes, perhaps this was all a clever, fuck-you, artistic ruse to get back at film critics. But at the end of the day, it is a movie. It is supposed to make us feel, or laugh, or cry, or really anything. In looking at my dad’s Lady in the Water post, it’s clear he was able to see Shyamalan for something that I can’t. I appreciate his sincerity and belief in this man, but I’m not sure I can see it. At least not for something like Lady in the Water.
